üìà How These Models Connect to Kaplan-Meier Analysis

üî¨ Step 1: Kaplan-Meier Baseline

The Kaplan-Meier estimator creates an empirical survival curve from your historical data by calculating the probability of 'survival' (not yet collected) at each time point. This gives us the raw S(t) = P(not collected by time t).

üìä Step 2: Collection Probability

We convert survival to collection probability: P(t) = 1 - S(t). This transforms the K-M curve into collection probability over time, which is what we want to model.

üéØ Step 3: Parametric Fitting

Each parametric model attempts to fit a mathematical function to the K-M derived collection probabilities. This allows smooth prediction and extrapolation beyond observed data.

üèÜ Step 4: Model Selection

We compare how well each parametric model captures the shape and behavior of your specific K-M curve using R¬≤, RMSE, and AIC metrics to choose the best fit.
